Loopsheets — colour a song for your loop station

Loop pedals are great fun and total chaos. The idea here: define a handful of small bars — each played on the guitar, drums, piano, or sung — and give every one a colour. Then write the song with coloured chips above the lyrics so you always know which loops should be running. A songbook for loop people.

How to read a loopsheet

  1. Pick your loops. Each card below is a short repeating bar — drums, bass, a piano figure, a guitar chord wash, a vocal pad, a shaker. Every loop has its own colour.
  2. Learn the loops first. Practise each one alone until the timing is comfortable. The notation under each card shows what's actually played.
  3. Read the song top-to-bottom. Each line of lyrics has a row of small bars above it. Inside each bar are coloured squares showing which loops are running at that moment.
  4. Stack and drop on the down-beat. When a new colour appears, you punch that loop in. When a colour disappears, you mute it. The loop station handles the rest.
  5. Hit play below to follow along — the active bar lights up so you don't lose your place.

The loop library

Six little bars, four bars long each, in the key of A minor at 92 BPM. Pick the ones you can play, ignore the rest, swap colours to taste.

Red

Heartbeat drums

Drums · 4 bars · 92 BPM

A simple boom-bap. Kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4, light hat through. Record with a metronome click and engage the loop on the down-beat.

HH  x x x x x x x x
SN  . . o . . . o .
KK  o . . . o . . .
Blue

Walking bass

Bass guitar · 4 bars

Roots of the progression on beats 1 and 3, walking up to the next chord on beat 4. Keep it round and quiet — leave space for everything else.

| A . . B  | F . . G  |
| C . . D  | G . . A  |
Green

Piano chords

Piano · 4 bars

Block chords on beat 1 of every bar, held with a soft sustain pedal. The harmonic backbone everything else leans on.

| Am  | F   | C   | G   |
  o.    o.    o.    o.
Yellow

Jangly guitar

Acoustic guitar · 4 bars

Up-strums on the off-beats over the same Am – F – C – G progression. Think tambourine, but with strings. Capo 2 if it sits high in your voice.

  &  &  &  &
  ↑  ↑  ↑  ↑   per bar
Purple

"Ooh" vocal pad

Voice into loop pedal · 4 bars

Hold an "ooh" on the third of each chord (C – A – E – B). Sing one bar at a time and let the loop overdub stack the harmony.

| C   | A   | E   | B   |
  ~~~   ~~~   ~~~   ~~~
Orange

Shaker & claps

Hand percussion · 4 bars

Sixteenth-note shaker, claps on beats 2 and 4. The "energy" loop — only bring it in when the song really lifts.

SH  xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
CL  . . o . . . o .
