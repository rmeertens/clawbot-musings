Loopsheets — "Black Horse and the Cherry Tree" as a colour map

KT Tunstall's Black Horse and the Cherry Tree is the song that put loop pedals on prime-time TV. On Later… with Jools Holland in 2004 she built it live with an Akai E2 Headrush — body percussion first, then a low-string riff, then the "Woo hoo!" hook, then she sang over the top. This page turns that build into a sheet you can read: every loop has a colour, every bar shows which colours should be running.

How to read a loopsheet

  1. Pick your loops. Each card below is a short repeating bar — drums, bass, a piano figure, a guitar chord wash, a vocal pad, a shaker. Every loop has its own colour.
  2. Learn the loops first. Practise each one alone until the timing is comfortable. The notation under each card shows what's actually played.
  3. Read the song top-to-bottom. Each line of lyrics has a row of small bars above it. Inside each bar are coloured squares showing which loops are running at that moment.
  4. Stack and drop on the down-beat. When a new colour appears, you punch that loop in. When a colour disappears, you mute it. The loop station handles the rest.
  5. Hit play below to follow along — the active bar lights up so you don't lose your place.

The loop library

Six little bars, four bars long each, key of E minor, around 110 BPM. Loops 1–3 are exactly what KT records on stage. Loops 4–6 are optional layers you can add if you have a band — drums, piano, or extra hands — to fill the song out.

Red

Body percussion

Acoustic guitar body · 4 bars · 110 BPM

Slap the soundboard near the bridge for the kick, tap your fingertips on the upper bout for the snare. This is the very first loop KT records — counted in by tapping the body before she's even strummed a note.

SLAP  o . . . o . . .
TAP   . . o . . . o .
Blue

Low-string riff

Acoustic guitar · 2 bars (loop ×2)

The descending hook on the low strings — open E, open D, the C and B on the A string. Palm-mute it lightly so it sits under everything else.

B|---------------|
G|---------------|
D|------0--------|

Green

"Woo hoo!" hook

Voice into loop pedal · 2 bars

The signature call: "Woo hoo, woo hoo!" on the off-beats of bar 1, then again in bar 2. Sing it once, overdub a harmony a fourth above on the second pass for that wall-of-vocals feel.

| .  woo  .  hoo | .  woo  .  hoo |
Yellow

Chord strums

Acoustic guitar · 4 bars

Down-up strums on the open chords — Em throughout the verse, lifting to G / D / A in the chorus. Bring this in only when you start singing live over the loops.

| Em        | Em        |
| G   D   A | G   D   A |
  ↓ ↑ ↓ ↑     ↓ ↑ ↓ ↑
Purple

Piano stabs

Piano (band-version layer) · 4 bars

Optional — for a fuller arrangement. Hit Em / G / D / A as short staccato chords on beat 1 of each bar. Adds bite without crowding the riff.

| Em  | G   | D   | A   |
  o.    o.    o.    o.
Orange

Stomp & clap

Foot stomp + claps · 4 bars

If you have an audience, get them clapping on 2 and 4. Otherwise loop a stomp box for the kick and your own claps for the snare. Bring it in for the final chorus.

STOMP  o . . . o . . .
CLAP   . . o . . . o .

The song — "Black Horse and the Cherry Tree"

Read top to bottom, left to right. Each row of small boxes is a span of bars; the coloured squares inside each box are the loops that should be running. The verse and chorus lyric lines below are placeholders — singing along is on you. The "Woo hoo!" hook is laid out verbatim because it is the song.
