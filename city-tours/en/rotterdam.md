# Half-Day City Tour: Rotterdam

**Duration:** ~4 hours  
**Best time:** Any day; Tuesday and Saturday have a market at Binnenrotte  
**Transport:** Metro and walking

## Overview

Rotterdam is unlike any other Dutch city. Almost entirely destroyed in the 1940 bombing, it rebuilt itself as a showcase of bold modern architecture. The result is one of Europe's most exciting urban landscapes: cube houses, a market hall shaped like a horseshoe, a pencil-shaped tower, and Europe's largest port stretching to the horizon. This tour covers the architectural highlights and the lively waterfront.

---

## Route

### 1. Markthal (20 minutes)
Start at the **Markthal**, Rotterdam's jaw-dropping indoor market hall (2014). The horseshoe-shaped building arches 40 metres over stalls selling food from around the world, while the interior walls are covered with a 11,000 m² digital artwork of giant fruit and vegetables. Grab breakfast from one of the stalls.

### 2. Cube Houses (Kubuswoningen) (20 minutes)
A short walk from the Markthal brings you to the **Cube Houses** designed by Piet Blom in 1984. Each home is a cube tilted 45 degrees on a hexagonal pylon — surreal to look at, surprisingly liveable inside. The **Kijk-Kubus** show house lets you see the interior.

### 3. Oudehaven & the Old Harbour (20 minutes)
Walk down to the **Oudehaven**, the oldest harbour in Rotterdam. Historic vessels are moored here beside the **Witte Huis** (White House, 1898) — once the tallest office building in Europe. The view across the water to the Erasmusbrug (Swan Bridge) is one of Rotterdam's best.

### 4. Erasmusbrug & Wilhelminapier (30 minutes)
Cross the elegant **Erasmusbrug** on foot for a panoramic view of the Maas river and the skyline. On the south bank, the **Wilhelminapier** is a cluster of iconic towers: the **Hotel New York** (former Holland-America Line HQ), the **De Rotterdam** mixed-use tower, and the **Montevideo** residential tower. Have a coffee at Hotel New York's terrace overlooking the river.

> The Hotel New York was where tens of thousands of European emigrants boarded ships to America in the early 20th century.

### 5. Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen Depot (1 hour)
Return to the north bank and visit the **Depot Boijmans Van Beuningen** — the world's first publicly accessible art depot, opened in 2021. The mirror-bowl-shaped building stores 151,000 artworks and allows visitors to walk among the actual storage racks. The rooftop garden and café offer great skyline views. (The main museum is under renovation until ~2028.)

## Getting There

Rotterdam Centraal is a 10-minute metro ride from the city centre highlights. Direct intercity trains from Amsterdam (1h15min), Den Haag (25 min), and Utrecht (40 min).
