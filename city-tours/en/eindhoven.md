# Half-Day City Tour: Eindhoven

**Duration:** ~4 hours  
**Best time:** Any day; Saturday market on the Markt  
**Transport:** Walking

## Overview

Eindhoven is the Netherlands' design capital — home to Philips, the Dutch Design Week (the largest design event in northern Europe), and a thriving creative scene. Unlike most Dutch cities it has virtually no medieval heritage: heavily bombed in WWII and rebuilt from scratch, Eindhoven embraced its industrial and design identity. The result is an energetic, forward-looking city with excellent contemporary culture.

---

## Route

### 1. Strijp-S (1 hour)
Start at **Strijp-S**, the former Philips factory complex on the western edge of the centre. Philips manufactured radios, televisions, and light bulbs here for decades. Now the red-brick industrial halls house design studios, galleries, start-ups, cafés, and music venues. Walk through the **Klokgebouw** (Clock Building) and explore the open courtyards. The scale of the complex — entire city blocks — conveys Eindhoven's industrial past.

> The **Dutch Design Week** takes place here every October, drawing 350,000 visitors.

### 2. Van Abbemuseum (1.5 hours)
Walk into the city centre to the **Van Abbemuseum**, one of Europe's leading contemporary art museums. Founded in 1936, it has an exceptional collection of modern and contemporary art including major works by El Lissitzky, Picasso, Joseph Beuys, and Marlene Dumas. The original 1936 building by A.J. Kropholler was expanded dramatically in 2003 by Abel Cahen, creating a striking interplay of old and new.

### 3. Wilhelminaplein & city centre (20 minutes)
Walk through the **Wilhelminaplein**, Eindhoven's main square with the landmark **Blob** and **Bubble** shopping buildings (designed by Massimiliano Fuksas). These playful, organic-shaped structures are emblematic of Eindhoven's refusal to build conventionally. Grab lunch at one of the square's restaurants.

### 4. Philips Museum (1 hour)
End at the **Philips Museum** in the company's original factory building from 1891 — where Gerard Philips started making light bulbs. The collection traces Philips's journey from a small light bulb factory to a global electronics company, with original products from every decade: early radios, televisions, electric shavers, medical equipment, and the Philips Compact Cassette. Engaging and nostalgic for anyone who grew up with consumer electronics.

> The museum is an easy 10-minute walk from the Van Abbemuseum.

## Getting There

Direct intercity trains from Amsterdam (1h20min), Utrecht (50 min), and Den Haag (1h30min). Eindhoven Airport serves budget carriers to many European destinations.
