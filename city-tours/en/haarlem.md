# Half-Day City Tour: Haarlem

**Duration:** ~4 hours  
**Best time:** Morning; Saturday has the best market  
**Transport:** Walking

## Overview

Haarlem is Amsterdam's elegant neighbour — just 20 minutes by train — with a beautiful medieval centre, one of the finest small art museums in the world, and a lively market square. It attracts far fewer tourists than Amsterdam, making for a more relaxed experience. The city was the home of Frans Hals and a centre of Dutch Golden Age painting.

---

## Route

### 1. Grote Markt (20 minutes)
Arrive at the **Grote Markt**, Haarlem's main square, dominated by the massive **Grote Kerk van Sint-Bavo** (St. Bavo's Church). On Saturdays a vibrant market fills the square with flowers, cheese, and street food. The 15th-century church is one of the largest in the Netherlands.

> The famous Müller organ inside Sint-Bavo was played by the young Mozart in 1766. Handel also visited. Free to enter the church.

### 2. Grote Kerk van Sint-Bavo (30 minutes)
Step inside **Sint-Bavo** to see the soaring Gothic interior and the magnificent 1738 **Christian Müller organ** — 5,000 pipes, 60 stops, and 30 metres tall. Organ recitals take place on Tuesday and Thursday afternoons in summer.

### 3. Teylers Museum (1.5 hours)
A 5-minute walk from the Grote Markt is the **Teylers Museum**, the oldest museum in the Netherlands (founded 1784). The neoclassical oval hall is stunning in itself. The collection spans fossils, scientific instruments, minerals, and Old Master drawings including works by Michelangelo and Raphael. Entry is excellent value for what you see.

### 4. Spaarne Riverbank (20 minutes)
Walk along the **Spaarne** river behind Teylers. The river frontage with the old warehouse cranes and the Waag (weigh house) is one of the most picturesque stretches in Haarlem. Cross the **Gravestenenbrug** swing bridge for the best view.

### 5. Frans Hals Museum (1 hour)
End at the **Frans Hals Museum** in the Oudemannenhuis, the former almshouse where the painter lived his final years. The museum houses the largest collection of Hals's group portraits — monumental canvases of Haarlem's civic guards, painted with a loose, almost modern brushstroke that was centuries ahead of its time.

> The second site of the Frans Hals Museum (Hal) is on the Grote Markt side if you prefer to end back near the station.

## Getting There

Haarlem station is a 10-minute walk from the Grote Markt. Direct trains run every 10–15 minutes from Amsterdam Centraal.
