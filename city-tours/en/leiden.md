# Half-Day City Tour: Leiden

**Duration:** ~4 hours  
**Best time:** Morning (start 9:30)  
**Transport:** Walking

## Overview

Leiden is a university city with a medieval heart, a network of quiet canals, and an extraordinary concentration of world-class museums. It was the birthplace of Rembrandt van Rijn and home to the oldest university in the Netherlands (1575). The city has a relaxed, intellectual atmosphere and is famous for its spring bulb fields just outside the city.

---

## Route

### 1. Burcht van Leiden (15 minutes)
Start at the **Burcht**, a circular 12th-century fortress on an artificial mound at the confluence of the two Rhine branches (the Oude and Nieuwe Rijn). Climb up for a panoramic view over the rooftops and canals. Entry is free.

### 2. Pieterskerk & Pilgrim Fathers (20 minutes)
Walk down to the **Pieterskerk**, the Gothic church at the heart of the old town. The **Pilgrim Fathers** — the English Separatists who later sailed on the *Mayflower* — lived in Leiden from 1609 to 1620. A small museum in the church documents their time here.

> Rembrandt was born a few streets away in 1606. A plaque marks the site of his birthplace on **Weddesteeg**.

### 3. Rapenburg Canal & Academy Building (20 minutes)
Stroll along the **Rapenburg**, widely considered the most beautiful canal in the Netherlands. The serene tree-lined waterway is flanked by the classical facades of Leiden University. The **Academiegebouw** (main university building) stands at the water's edge.

### 4. Rijksmuseum van Oudheden (Egyptian Museum) (1.5 hours)
On the Rapenburg you'll find the **Rijksmuseum van Oudheden** — the national museum of antiquities. The centrepiece is the **Temple of Taffeh**, a complete 1st-century AD Egyptian temple donated by Egypt to the Netherlands in 1979. The museum also holds extensive Greek, Roman, and pre-historic Dutch collections.

> This is one of the finest antiquity museums in northern Europe and often overlooked in favour of Amsterdam.

### 5. Hortus Botanicus Leiden (45 minutes)
End your tour at the **Hortus Botanicus**, one of the oldest botanical gardens in the world (founded 1590). It played a key role in introducing tulips to Europe from Turkey. The glasshouses contain tropical and sub-tropical plants; the reconstructed 17th-century garden shows what Carolus Clusius planted here.

## Getting There

Intercity Direct trains from Amsterdam Centraal (35 min) and Den Haag Centraal (15 min). Leiden Centraal is also on the Schiphol–Rotterdam high-speed line.
